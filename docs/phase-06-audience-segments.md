# Phase 6 — Audience-segment sections (stag parties + corporate)

Arlindo called out two customer segments the current page does not address at all. Both are meaningful chunks of the business.

## Files

- `app/page.tsx` — two new sections (or a single section with two side-by-side cards).

## Segments

### 1. Stag / bachelor parties

- Group photos of people "having fun" — Arlindo said the gallery has these.
- Speaks to teenagers and 20-somethings — the buyers of the 1 h / 1 h 30 tours.
- CTA: `PLAN YOUR PARTY` → contact form with pre-filled `experience=Stag Party` or a message pre-fill.
- Copy angle: "Bachelor / stag weekends, birthdays, big-group adventures. Tell us the group size and date, we'll build the day around it."

### 2. Corporate events

- Arlindo: "very important" and "an easy sell" — the company pays.
- Positioned as the natural home for the Jeep + wine tasting product (Phase 5).
- Groups often 20–50 people — highlight this capacity.
- Mention the "mix" option: some of the group on quads with Guincho Adventours, others on the Jeep tour with the partner — meets the "we don't have that many quads" objection Arlindo flagged.
- CTA: `REQUEST A CORPORATE PROPOSAL` → contact form with pre-filled `experience=Corporate Event`.
- Copy angle: "Off-sites, team-building days, incentive trips. Quads on the coast, Jeeps to Sintra + wine cellars, or a mix of both. Groups from 10 to 50+."

## Layout

Two-up section between Reviews and the Booking form. Each card has an eyebrow, headline, three bullet lines, and a CTA.

## Acceptance

- Both segments have a distinct visual block on the page.
- Booking form pre-fills the Experience field when the user arrives from either CTA.
- Booking form's Experience dropdown adds two new options: `Stag Party` and `Corporate Event`.

## Blockers

- Real photos of groups. Arlindo said the gallery has them — Phase 11.
